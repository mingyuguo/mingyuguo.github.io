Profile

About Me

I am a Senior Lecturer in the School of Computer Science and Information Technology, Adelaide University.

My research focuses on building intelligent systems for strategic decision-making in complex, multiagent environments. For example, I leverage algorithmic game theory and optimisation to design coordination strategies for massive-scale autonomous systems, such as sovereign drone swarms. In cybersecurity, my work combines algorithmic graph-pattern exploration with machine learning to identify high-risk attack paths and strategically deploy defences within enterprise networks.

My ongoing research investigates the role of Large Language Models (LLMs) as the backbone for strategic reasoning and automated decision-making. This includes developing agentic workflows that optimise reasoning quality under strict cost constraints and utilising LLMs as automated heuristic designers for complex optimisation applications. I also study game-theoretical applications to GenAI-era economic models, including agentic commerce, autonomous economic decision-making, automated negotiation, advertisement integration, and market-based LLM provisioning.

Prior to joining the school, I was a Lecturer at the University of Liverpool, UK. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Duke University, USA. My Ph.D. Dissertation was recognised as the runner-up for the Victor Lessor Distinguished Dissertation Award. I hold a M.S. degree in Applied Mathematics from University of Florida, USA and a B.S. degree in Mathematics (Chu Kochen Honour College) from Zhejiang University, China.
